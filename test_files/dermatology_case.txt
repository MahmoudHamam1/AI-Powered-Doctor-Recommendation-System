DERMATOLOGY CONSULTATION REPORT

Patient: Jane Smith
Date: January 20, 2024

PRESENTING COMPLAINT:
Patient presents with severe itching and red rashes on both arms and legs that have persisted for 2 weeks. The rashes are raised, scaly, and worsen at night. Patient reports no known allergies but recently started using a new laundry detergent.

PHYSICAL EXAMINATION:
- Multiple erythematous patches on bilateral extremities
- Dry, flaky skin with excoriation marks
- No signs of infection

ASSESSMENT:
Possible contact dermatitis or eczema

TREATMENT PLAN:
1. Discontinue new laundry detergent
2. Topical corticosteroid cream
3. Antihistamine for itching
4. Follow-up in 1 week
